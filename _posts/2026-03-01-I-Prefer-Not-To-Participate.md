## Why I'm Here

Since I've started university again many questions about the future were pushed off to a later date because I thought pragmatically that is a later problem and with things so far away why consider them now. I turn 25 in a couple of months and this is supposed to be where my brain stops developing and it all goes down hill or something? I'm graduating at the end of this year and unfortunately my want to be ambitious and dedicate so many concentrated back to back months to this school has awarded me with less time for those considerations which were due later. 

I haven't been able to sleep lately, I thought it was because there's so much work due and so many projects, and personal things happening all at once. But that's not really been a bother before. I mean yeah sure I've never slept much during times like these but when I did there was quality to the few hours I could catch. It feels like my brain is on fire at night and all the melatonin has done is pour gasoline on my nightmares, which is also not conducive to good sleep (I've stopped the melatonin). I've been done with all the work necessary to enjoy my spring break early for days and I still haven't been able to do so, why? 

My conscious has been getting ripped apart, dealing with all sorts of moral questions about the world, the place of AI in it, who are the men selling our souls to the damned? Questions of existential dread I feel most overthinkers in my age group tend to have. During one of these spirals I was reading the news regarding Anthropic & the Pentagon's big feud over a domestic surveillance state, and fully autonomous weapons. I was surprised that they would be willing to take the kind of damage the government was threatening to impose on them if they did not comply. I've been very nihilistic about the world and life in general. To be clear, I'm not saying their feud gave me hope and motivated me to be more optimistic about the future or anything like that. I am aware that Anthropic just lost 200 million in a contract but their gain in reputation amongst the technocrats will likely be worth much more than that, I do feel their was a calculated risk to this decision which is not as altruistic as they make it seem. Or maybe that's the nihilism again. 

---
## The Mil Industrial Complex, Engineers, and Me Walk Into a Bar...

Anyways, that made me think of my own relation to the government and my future career. How do I want to participate in the world of engineering? People are quick to sign their lives away for six figures to any company regardless of what they contribute to it. There's all kinds of justifications and levels of removal one will do when it comes to these type of things. Mental gymnastics so good they would win gold in the Olympics; don't get me wrong I am judging with no ground to stand on. This same question has been causing these fires in my head but unfortunately my gymnastics wouldn't even qualify for a medal, as no amount of justification seems to satisfy the judge lineup that makes my moral compass. I'm not contributing to weapons of mass destruction, so it's okay right? I am feeding the industrial complex with the knowledge and skills learned through my work at the institution but I'm at such a low level, I won't contribute anything worth it right? It's only a couple of weeks so what would be the harm in it, it's just to buff the resume and then we'll find a better offer later, right? 

Needless to say, I unfortunately know the answer to all of these questions and they've been keeping me up. At first I was so giddy to have been given an opportunity to work for an established company, I mean little ole me work at one of those military contractors? I said yes and started going through the motions to get everything done that they needed from me. Fast forward to mid-January, I was talking to a friend of mine about it and they asked me why I wanted to work for that company and they called me out on my bullshit. I told them all about how I wanted to avoid contributing to the military industrial complex prior to receiving the offer or even applying. Which I responded to with all of my justifications, the reasons why it's different for me, why I am not **REALLY** contributing to the industrial military complex, how I'm an exception to the rule, rules for thee not for me. Which they called me out on again. With friends like these why have enemies? 

It was after this conversation that I think things started to take a turn for the worse; I actually started ***thinking about it***. I read headlines regarding our military engagements, I kept considering how my tax dollars go to fund these different operations; soon so will my labor. I spoke to that same friend about it again yesterday, and they made a very apt comparison. They compared my view of the world of AI and my view of those who work in defense. I view the majority of backers for this technology as used car salesmen, knowing little of their inventory while smiling at you telling you it's the best and to buy now to whatever slop they're pedaling. At least that market has the choice open to have ethically considerate people who are aware of how the technology works and push for guardrails. Defense has a much harder time making that argument, what do you think that drone you're making will do? What do you think the weapons system you develop will be used for? The various videos which circle social media of towns being leveled, imagine you sit there one day and go "I've seen this grainy look before". Maybe you'll get a cool anecdote you can tell your friends about how the reason the footage looks so poor is because the team you were on had a miscommunication email with another department and blah blah blah. Maybe by the end of your story the video of the leveled village will have looped a couple times and the platform will give you similar videos you can use to tell that story to different friend groups.

---
## So What Now?

Being a moral person who follows principles isn't easy and I by no means am saying I am some uber righteous guy who only walks on the right path. I haven't even hit 25 yet, maybe when the clock strikes midnight I'll go back quickly to this post and delete it going 180 on all my positions here. For the time being though, I am choosing to forego on an opportunity that is beneficial to the procurement of future stable and consistent employment. I understand why people pick the career path, why they work with these kinds of companies. Sometimes greed because they pay the most out of all of their offers and money is their only focus. Sometimes to just pay their bills because they only got an offer from a contractor company and those student loans are no joke. Sometimes because it is your way of contributing to the war effort because you like it and for some reason or another you couldn't join the military so you use your brain for it instead. There's many scenarios that apply for the reasoning of why people will overlook or do the aforementioned gymnastics. I just simply disagree with being part of that world and I would love to get some sleep; so I prefer not to participate.
