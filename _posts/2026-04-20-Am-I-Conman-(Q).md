## Oh The Lies We Tell

I am legitimately asking the question, because I am not intentionally trying to be a con man but as my circles state it seems I am on fraud watch. For some background when I was in the second grade I was held back due to failing English. It was my second language and I didn't have much exposure at home outside of the TV and my neighbors. I couldn't trace the stupid letters on those handouts that we were given, I couldn't spell, and words sounded really funny when I said them due to my thick accent. Think of what it might sound like if broken English and the Bronx African American community was the frame of reference for what the language was supposed to be. I skirted by in math barely passing.

Fast forward to my 7th grade English class where my teacher would say that I had a pretty good grasp of writing for my age and where I would find out I had a pretty advanced English reading level according to state exams (NOT A GOOD INDICATOR). Great, I am good at something! Then in 8th grade, a friend of mine was telling me to apply for this Rutgers Summer Scholars program. He said I'm smart so it would be easy for me to get in if I applied. If you got accepted you would spend a summer learning math and science stuff at Rutgers University, all you needed was to apply with your report card and all this other stuff they asked for maybe a test of some sort. I applied, and I got rejected due to my low performance in the STEM related subjects. I liked science class, learning about science stuff but I wasn't much good, and I hated math. It always seemed so complicated, all these numbers and silly things you needed to do like multiplication and division. Eventually my friend followed up with me when he got accepted and asked if I heard back and I told him I got in but couldn't afford it. I thought it cringe to admit that I got rejected for academics the very thing he said I was good at. Luckily this didn't become a trend in my life.

## I LIED IT BECAME A TREND

In the beginning of the middle school year, there was also High School applications going around for all these unique programs that were supposedly better than the public education available in my poor people town. I talked to a counselor at my school about applying for this and they said it was a great idea. This was before she saw my grades, at which point her tone shifted and gave me a look like she was going to tell me that my dog ran away after she Old Yeller-ed it in the yard moments prior. She said to me "maybe you can explain in your letter the difficulties in why your grades aren't doing so well." At the time this whole concept of needing good grades to get into something seemed silly, I mean they would be able to tell that I was a genius just by looking at my smart looking face. Okay I was dumb, can you blame me? My mom hadn't even graduated high school in her own country; nobody told me about any of this stuff. In spite of her demeanor I applied anyway.

On top of applying to local schools in Jersey, I applied to Bronx Science because my aunt told me about how my cousin had been accepted there the year prior and she could do some administrative trickery with her connections so that I could sit in for the SHSAT. Whatever she did ended up working and I gave it my all on the exam, as well as the applications. However, by the end of 8th grade I could say I empathized with George Bush. I knew what it felt like reading to those kids on 9/11 when that security detail filled him in. "Sir, Piscataway VOTECH has rejected you" 1 WTC down, "Sir, Bronx Science has rejected you." 2 WTC down, "Sir, Edison Academy has Rejected you" the pentagon is hit, you get the idea. By the end of my middle school I had 5 total rejections received and I was confused. How is everyone telling me I'm this smart kid but these schools don't seem to notice?

## Peaked In High School Type Beat

My freshman year of High School I was performing poorly in physics and math. I had a come to Jesus moment after my physics teacher gave a friend of mine and me a talking to about how academics is truly the only way to escape poverty. It was filled with rhetoric regarding how smart we were, and how we're not like the other students, we can truly succeed if we just try hard enough. Hey, it worked. Unfortunately, it was during the last half of the school year that I received this speech, but I started to lock-in for real nonetheless. I even cut a deal with both the math and physics teachers that if I could average out a B+ for the rest of the year, I would get a recommendation to summer schooling to skip a math class and get into Honors Chemistry the next year. An important note as I wouldn't meet the normal requirements for either of these cases as I had gotten a D for both classes within the first quarter. They agreed, and I did both of those things. 

Sophomore year I was involved in Debate, tried starting my own club, and tried focusing on tech as my future was going to be in computer science (AT THE TIME A GOOD IDEA). I learned about politics, ethics, and history in this year from debate and I was decent at all of that stuff, I think. For some reason I was still got C in Algebra 2 and a B- in Chemistry, improvement but I needed to get all As because I knew colleges wanted a high GPA. The only good that came from that misery was knowledge of what to aim for. Maybe I just wasn't hungry enough; after finals there I was middle of the rings again. For some reason, this is when I started to now hear from my educators as well, that they were surprised I wasn't achieving the marks becoming of a genius like me.

Junior year I followed the same trend, B- in Biology, C- in Calculus, A in English, A in Philosophy. What the hell is going on? Why couldn't I be good at the subjects I wanted to be good in? Now, philosophy was fun, and I also took the only CS related class in the school and got an A- in it (Teacher was a dick, hence the minus). AND AGAIN! All these adults kept saying, maybe you're not applying yourself sufficiently, maybe those exams were a fluke. They were coping on my behalf, and I just agreed. I started thinking school was dumb, all of this college that college this, unnecessary. This was of course after I applied to an MIT program in the summer; I told a friend of mine and when I went to ask her if she had heard back after my rejection, she told me she was put on the waitlist. She eventually got into the summer program and ended up graduating last year from the school. Fuck.

## Is Academia My Guilty Pleasure?

My first time trying to go to college (shit happens), I had to take a summer semester that was for the poor kids called EOF summer, we took classes on social studies, and something else I can't remember. I did really well in these courses I believe I got a 4.0. My ego was imploding when I was hearing the professor say that I had a "unique and strong voice" in my writing. When the Fall semester came, I took a philosophy class. It was difficult and required heavy reading, and I never really did that before on my own, so I had to learn what studying actually meant. I had to take pre-calculus again because apparently I just had the look of someone who didn't get it the first time. They were right, I got a C. My 1 CS class I got an A- in, and I also had to take some other social studies class, which... it was just reading text and responding online and I met the professor once. COVID schooling was evil. My advisor had a 1 on 1 with me at the end of the semester and told me something along the lines of "Interesting, why didn't you get a 4.0 this semester Angel? You know that's where everyone needs to be at right?" Finally a person who wasn't telling me that they expected ME to get it instead, now it was everyone's mark and I wasn't there! I responded to him so gleefully "I don't know math is not really a good subject for me." to which he responded "Maybe this degree isn't for you then, you will only continue to face challenges pertaining to math and logic, how do you expect to be better?" He got me there.

Anyways, I then dropped out of college and went to the Navy, unrelated to that, and more so that COVID is going to last forever and online schooling was an evil thing. Instead of choosing easy jobs with at most a 6 month school house like 99% of all jobs in the military, the guy whose been historically bad at school says NAH. I choose to go to the Naval Nuclear Program, the Electrician's Mate Nuclear rate, with a projected School time of 2 years. Something, is wrong with me!!! I can't talk much about the topics that I did in the nuclear program, but I'll say this. There's a saying regarding the GPA everyone in the lower totem poles of the academics would repeat "2.5 stay alive"; I stayed alive. Even there was another drove of people telling me they thought I would be performing better than I was. Complete strangers. 

Now in my present circumstance at the precious Iron Maiden of a school, RPI. I threw myself back into academia under terrible circumstances. I had bills to pay living on my own and such, so I needed a full time job, can't do a full time job during the day that's class time. SOLUTION! Work the night shift after you get out of class, then after your shift is over back to class. GENIUS! I performed so poorly for 2 semesters straight that I was at risk of being academically suspended. I finally worked something out regarding being able to afford living and also do schooling full-time midway through my second semester. So then I left my job and I could focus full-time on my academics. I channeled the maximum amount of energy I could into these classes during the summer, I rarely socialized outside of class, I had back to back all-nighters studying for exams, I was drowning in work, and I was also dealing with personal life problems by just ignoring them completely. After all that work and effort. I ended in the semester making the Dean's list. Finally, the prophecy came true.
